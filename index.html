Jamaica Travel, Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time to visit Jamaica?

The best time to visit Jamaica is November through mid-December and January through April, the dry season, with warm temperatures, low humidity, and minimal rain. Peak season runs December to April, so book accommodation early. Summer (June to August) is hot and humid but sees far fewer tourists and lower prices, which many locals prefer.

What are the best things to do in Jamaica beyond the resorts?

Jamaica rewards those who venture past the all-inclusive. Top experiences include hiking to Blue Mountain Peak at sunrise, rafting down the Rio Grande, swimming in the luminous lagoon in Falmouth, exploring YS Falls in St Elizabeth, eating escovitch fish in Port Royal, and catching a local dancehall session in Kingston. TRODDR maps all of these with insider tips.

Where should I eat in Jamaica like a local?

Skip the hotel buffet. Jamaica's real food scene is at roadside jerk centres, fish fry spots along the Hellshire and Middle Quarters routes, and family-run eateries in every parish. Kingston has the most diverse restaurant scene, from traditional Jamaican breakfast spots to Chinese-Jamaican cook shops and modern Caribbean fine dining. TRODDR curates the best local restaurants across the island.

Is Jamaica safe for tourists?

Jamaica is a welcoming country and millions of visitors travel safely every year. Like any destination, knowing where to go and getting local guidance makes a significant difference. TRODDR's recommendations are curated with this in mind, and every spot is a place locals are comfortable bringing visitors to.
